Clarence Seedorf is a former Dutch professional football player with an estimated net worth of $56.2 million.

Clarence Seedorf started playing football at an early age of 6 when he joined VV AS '80 and Real Almere. In 1983, he was recruited by the AFC Ajax to which he continued to play with until he stepped into the senior division of the team.

He was the youngest player to ever joined the Ajax at age 16 when he turned professional on November 29, 1992. Seedorf led the team to win the Dutch Cup, Dutch Super Cup and Eredivisie during the 1993-1994 season. He didn't extend his stay with Ajax which he moved to Sampdoria in 1995.

While with Sampdoria, Seedorf appeared in 32 matches but he secured his transfer to Real Madrid by the end of the season. Other teams that he played with include Internazionale, Milan, and Botafogo. He became the coach of A.C. Milan, after he ended his playing career. After his four-month stint, he was dismissed by the team.

Clarence Clyde Seedorf was born on April 1, 1976 in Paramaribo, Suriname. He is married to Luviana and have four children.
