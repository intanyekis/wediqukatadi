A Look At Aaron Paul's Incredible Career In Hollywood

Hey there fellow entertainment enthusiasts, let's take a break from our binge-watching sessions and dive into the captivating world of Aaron Paul's journey in Hollywood! Let's explore how this dynamic actor transformed from a small-town boy into a Hollywood sensation. From his humble beginnings to his explosive breakthrough role, Aaron Paul's career has been nothing short of incredible. So, get ready to be amazed by this actor's dynamic range, mesmerizing talent, and undeniable charm. Without further ado, let's take a peek into Aaron Paul's life and see how he has taken the industry by storm!

The Nascent of the Star Called Aaron Paul

Via Flickr/Kinematografija Social

Aaron Paul's journey from Emmett, Idaho to Hollywood was nothing short of a wild ride. Born a month prematurely in his parents' bathroom, he was destined for greatness from the start. Growing up, he participated in church plays, honing his acting skills before making the move to the big city. Armed with his trusty 1982 Toyota Corolla and $6,000 in savings, he hit the road with his mom and never looked back. His first foray into the entertainment industry came in the form of an appearance on The Price Is Right, where he, unfortunately, overbid on his Showcase.

Undeterred, he took on a job as a movie theater usher at Universal Studios, where he would eventually become a star. Aaron is also known for his love life, having met his wife Lauren Parsekian at Coachella and marrying her in a 1920s Parisian carnival-themed wedding, complete with Foster the People and John Mayer performances. When he's not busy being a family man, Aaron can be found indulging in his passion for mezcal. With a life like his, it's no wonder that Governor Butch Otter declared October 1 the Aaron Paul Sturtevant Day.

Aaron Paul’s Early Days in Hollywood

Via Flickr/Kinematografija Social

Aaron Paul’s early days in Hollywood were anything but conventional. He took the modeling world by storm, winning runner-up at the International Modeling and Talent Association competition in Los Angeles in 1996. Shortly after, he landed gigs in music videos, which ultimately led to appearances in several TV commercials. With his acting career on the rise, Paul started appearing in films like Whatever It Takes, Help! I'm a Fish, and National Lampoon's Van Wilder.

However, it wasn't until he played the role of Jesse Pinkman in Breaking Bad that he became a household name. Paul's performance as a meth addict was so convincing that it earned him three Primetime Emmy Awards. When not busy acting, Paul can be found cheering on his beloved Boise State Broncos. Whether he is playing a character or supporting his favorite football team, Aaron Paul is always one to watch.

Aaron At His Peak

Via Flickr/Kinematografija Social

From meth nephew on SNL to starring as a street racer in Need for Speed, Aaron Paul has had one heck of a ride. Let's not forget his role as the drunken father in Hellion or as Joshua in Exodus: Gods and Kings. But his career reached new heights when he took on the role of Eddie Lane in The Path, questioning his faith as he joins a cult. Now, he's set to star in the upcoming season of Black Mirror, adding another notch to his acting belt.

Paul is, however, not just limited to the big and small screens. He's lent his voice to Unity and Kingsglaive: Final Fantasy XV, and even made a surprise appearance on WWE Raw to promote Need for Speed. Plus, with roles in crime dramas like Truth Be Told and Westworld, it's clear that Paul's acting range knows no bounds. This actor's journey is far from over!

Paul’s Other Ventures In Hollywood

Via Flickr/Kinematografija Social

When Paul isn't busy stealing the show in movies and TV, he's out there making a difference in the world, one contest and bottle of mezcal at a time. In 2013, he helped his wife's non-profit anti-bullying organization raise $1.8 million by organizing a contest, with the prize being a trip to the Hollywood Forever Cemetery screening of the final episode of Breaking Bad. Talk about a killer finale experience!

As if that wasn't impressive enough, in 2019, Paul and his Breaking Bad co-star Bryan Cranston decided to give their fans something else to obsess over besides their iconic performances. They launched their own line of mezcal called Dos Hombres, because who needs a hit show when you can have a hit liquor? It's no wonder Paul's career is at its peak; he knows how to keep his fans entertained both on and off the screen.

Aaron Paul's Awards And Recognitions

Via Wikipedia Commons

Aaron Paul's trophy cabinet is as full as his acting chops. From his first Primetime Emmy nomination in 2009 for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series for his role in Breaking Bad, he has been racking up nods and wins like a pro. He won the Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actor on Television in 2009 and 2011, and the Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Drama Series in 2014.

In 2019, his work on El Camino: A Breaking Bad Movie won him a Satellite Award for Best Actor in a Miniseries or TV Film, and in 2020, his movie, also a Breaking Bad spin-off, took home the Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Movie Made for Television. If you're keeping count, that's a whopping five awards from different organizations for his time as Jesse Pinkman alone! And he has a lot more awards and nominations for his role in other movies. It's no surprise that he's become a seasoned pro at delivering acceptance speeches, and his charm and wit always shine through.
