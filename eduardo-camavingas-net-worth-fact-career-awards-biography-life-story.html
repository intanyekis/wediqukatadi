Eduardo Camavinga is a French professional football player who plays for Ligue 1 club Rennes and the France national team. He started playing football for the youth team of Drapeau- Fougeres in 2009. In 2010, at the age of 11, he joined the youth team of Rennes where he signed his first professional contract on 14 December 2018, at the age of 16 years and one month, becoming the youngest ever professional for the club. He made his professional debut for Rennes on 6 April 2019 and became the youngest player to ever play for Rennes’ first team at 16 years and 4 months old.

On the international level, he has represented France at various age levels. He has represented France at U21 and the senior team. He made his France national team debut on 8 September 2020 where he became the youngest player to play for the French national team since Maurice Gastiger in 1914 at 17 years, 9 months, and 29 days old.

Eduardo Camavinga Club Career

  • He started playing football for the youth team of Drapeau- Fougeres in 2009.

Eduardo Camavinga International Career

  • He has represented France at various age levels. 
  • He has represented France at U21 and the senior team.
  • On 27 August 2020, he was called up to play on France’s senior team after Paul Pogba was sidelined by a positive COVID-19 test where he became the youngest player to be called up to the French senior team since Rene Gerard in 1932, who was only 17 years, 9 months and 17 days old.
  • He made his France national team debut on 8 September 2020 in a 4-2 win against Croatia in the UEFA Nations League, replacing N’Golo Kante midway through the second half where he became the youngest player to play for the French national team since Maurice Gastiger in 1914 at 17 years, 9 months, and 29 days old.
  • On 7 October 2020, he made his first start for France and scored his first international goal with an overhead kick in a 7-1 win against Ukraine which made him the youngest goalscorer for France since Maurice Gastiger in 1914.
