Why Is Bruce Arians Retiring? History of Health Issues, Did The Cancer Come Back?

Bruce Arians, who led the Tampa Bay Buccaneers to a Super Bowl victory, announced that he is retiring as the coach of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers and fans are eager to know why he is retiring.

Bruce Charles Arians (born October 3, 1952) is a former head coach and current executive of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers of the National Football League (NFL).

On March 30, 2022, he announced his resignation from coaching and that he would be transferring to front office operations for the upcoming season. From 2013 until 2017, he was the head coach of the Arizona Cardinals.

Following Tom Brady's announcement in February that he was retiring, this is the Bucs' second huge retirement announcement this offseason.

Why Is Bruce Arians Retiring? Health and Illness

Bruce Arians, the former head coach of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, has elected to retire from his position as head coach and take on the job of senior football adviser in the front office. He says that he is not retiring due to his health issues but for personal reasons.

After retiring from the Cardinals after the 2017 season due to health reasons, Arians returned to coach the Buccaneers after a year away. Arians, who will age 70 this season, led the Buccaneers to their first Super Bowl triumph in 2020, which was Brady's first with the team.

Bruce was 31-18 in three seasons with the Buccaneers, and he was 80-48-1 overall in eight years as a head coach, including five seasons with the Cardinals from 2013 to 2017.

What happened to Bruce Arians? Did The Cancer Come Back?

Bruce Arians, a two-time cancer survivor, stood down as coach of the Arizona Cardinals in 2017 due to medical concerns, only to be rehired by the Buccaneers two years later. He is not suffering from cancer this time.

This time, he added, he's feeling better than he has in "many years" and is looking forward to working with general manager Jason Licht in the front office.

Bruce Arians Wife and Net Worth Explored

The wife of Bruce Arians is Christine Arians, and he has an estimated net worth of around $20 million.

The Arians Family Foundation, which Arians and his wife Christine operate, supports and creates initiatives to prevent and treat child abuse and neglect. The Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA) program is supported by the Arians Family Foundation.

He and his wife Christine have a son, Jake, who played placekicker for the Buffalo Bills for part of the 2001 season, and a daughter, Kristi Anne. Arians and his wife currently live in Tampa, Florida.
