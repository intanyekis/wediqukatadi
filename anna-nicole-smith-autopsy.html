Anna Nicole Smith Autopsy Revealed Her Exact Cause Of Death

Anna Nicole Smith autopsy showed why breast augmentation surgery is dangerous for health, and sometimes it can end up claiming your life.

The deceased was born in Houston in 1967 and had a very humble beginning.

Before finding fame, she worked multiple low-wage jobs, including waiting at Red Lobster, stints at Walmart, and a fried chicken restaurant.

Eventually, she decided to become a stripper in a Houston club to support her young son.

In 1992 she was featured on the Playboy magazine cover and won the Playmate of the Year award in 1993.

She signed modeling contracts with apparel monster H&M and Guess Jeans while also appearing in the comedy sequel ’Naked Gun 33 & A Third.’

Smith also appeared in her own reality show, ‘The Anna Nicole Show,’ which aired on E!.

Her show was controversial as Anna frequently seemed to be drunk during shooting, sometimes merely mumbling words.

Smith was also trolled for slurring while introducing Kanye West during the 2004 American Music Awards.

Where are Anna Nicole Smith children now?

Smith had only two kids named Dannielynn Birkhead and Daniel Wayne Smith. 

On September 10, 2006, her eldest son, Daniel, died from an accidental overdose at age 20.

Her long-time companion, Howard K. Stern said that she never recovered from Daniel’s sudden death.

Anna Nicole Smith daughter, Dannielynn, was born on September 7, 2006.

What led Anna Nicole Smith to prescription drugs addiction

Smith’s friend, Missy Byrum, claimed that her prescription drug addiction began after breast augmentation surgery in her early twenties.

Byrum named the drugs Vicodin, Xanax, Klonopin, Valium, and Lortabs as part of Anna’s drug abuse. 

She also stated that Anna was taking these drugs since the time of her surgery, and she could do nothing to stop it.

Missy claimed that in the early 1990s, Anna was a “full-blown drug addict,” and according to some reports, in 1994, she had survived an accidental overdose in a Hollywood hotel room.

Anna Nicole Smith found dead in Florida

Smith’s prescription drug addiction took her life. On February 8, 2007, she was dead in a hotel room.

The forensic pathologist and Broward County Medical Examiner, Joshua Perper, led the investigation of Anna Nicole Smith death and concluded that she died of “combined drug intoxication.”

Anna Nicole Smith autopsy report

Anna Nicole Smith autopsy reported that her death was a result of an accidental overdose of a sleeping pill and at least eight other prescriptions. 

She also had a bacterial infection from injecting medications into her body’s rear end.

Perper said that Smith died due to combined drug intoxication with the sleeping medicine chloral hydrate as the main factor.

Smith had been taking a long list of medicines, including methadone for valium and pain, but those medications were at a therapeutic level.

However, Chloral hydrate is a sedative used to treat alcohol withdrawal and insomnia, ease post-surgery pain, and relieve anxiety.

Dr. Chip Walls, a forensic toxicologist for the Miller School of Medicine at the University of Miami, said that the medicine is rarely prescribed and is known to be fatal if combined with other drugs, including the sedative Lorazepam, which the Anna Nicole Smith autopsy proved that she was taking.

Perper shared that she also had been on multiple antianxiety and antidepressant drugs and had taken longevity medicines, vitamin B12, and growth hormone.

According to an assistant medical examiner’s report, a table in Anna’s hotel room contained SlimFast, an open box of Tamiflu tablets, soda cans, cold medicine, and nicotine gum.
