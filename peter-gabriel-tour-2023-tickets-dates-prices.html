For the first time since 2012, Peter Gabriel is touring North America.

From Sept. 8 through Oct. 21, the 73-year-old former Genesis frontman and Rock and Roll Hall of Famer will play 25 arenas in the U.S. and Canada as part of his upcoming ‘i/o The Tour’ in support of his new album.

Midway through the short run of shows, the “Solsbury Hill” singer has two New York City show lined up — and they’re biggies.

On Sept. 18, Gabriel will play Madison Square Garden.

Then, four days later, he heads upstate for a huge arena show at Buffalo’s KeyBank Center on Sept. 22.

And if it’s been too long since you’ve heard “Sledgehammer,” “Shock The Monkey” and “In Your Eyes” live, we’re here to help you get your hands on tickets today.

Peter Gabriel new album

The tour is in support of ‘i/o,’ Gabriel’s first full-length album since 2011’s “New Blood.”

Thus far, he’s released a trio of singles “Panopticom,” “The Court” and “Playing For Time.”

Gabriel says the single “Playing For Time” is about “time, mortality and the idea that each of us has a planet full of memories which get stashed inside the brain.”

At the time of publication, there’s no release date for the new record.

That being said, Gabriel is known for putting music into the world based on the lunar calendar. “‘Playing For Time’ is no exception, dropping in line with the March full moon,” according to StereoBoard.

So, keep an eye on your lunar calendars — that just might be the day we finally hear the Peter Gabriel release we’ve been waiting on for over ten years.
